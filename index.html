Capabilities

Capture, record, and edit — one keyboard away

✂ Region capture

Freeze the entire screen, draw a selection box, and capture at native resolution. No window juggling.

✍️ Annotation tools

Rectangle, ellipse, arrow, line, and freehand pen. Annotate directly on the frozen capture before saving.

🎨 Color presets

Quick-switch between color presets for your annotations. Visual clarity without digging through a color picker.

↺ Undo & redo

Full history stack so you can back out of any annotation. Iterate until the capture tells the story you need.

⏺ One-key recording

Hit the hotkey, pick your region or go full screen, and start recording immediately. No setup dialogs.

🎬 30 or 60 fps

Choose between smooth 60 fps for detailed walkthroughs or lighter 30 fps for quick captures and smaller files.

🎤 System & mic audio

Capture system audio, microphone input, or both. Record exactly what you hear and say in one take.

💾 Native MP4

Records directly to MP4 with no post-processing or conversion step. Files are ready to share the moment you stop.

🎬 Screen → GIF

Record any screen or window, full-screen or a region, straight to an animated GIF — no external tools or ffmpeg.

⚙️ 15 / 30 / 60 fps

Pick a frame-rate preset or a custom value (5–60 fps). Frames are captured at the area's real on-screen resolution.

🎨 Accurate colors, small files

Per-frame 256-color palettes keep screen text crisp, and inter-frame differencing keeps the file size down.

💬 Prefer video? One click

Slack, GitHub and Jira autoplay video — sharper and smaller than a GIF. A nudge switches you to the recorder instantly.

📂 Open With → Snap It

Right-click any PNG, JPG or WebP in Finder and open it in Snap It — or use the tray's “Open image…” picker.

✍️ Same annotation tools

The whole image becomes the canvas, edited with the exact rectangle, ellipse, arrow, pen and color tools you use on screenshots.

💾 Save a copy or overwrite

Export a fresh copy by default, or overwrite the original after a confirmation — at native resolution, preserving the format.

🔑 Never the default handler

Snap It registers only as an editor, so it shows up in “Open With” without ever hijacking Preview or Photos.

🔒

Nothing leaves your machine. No accounts, no uploads, no telemetry. Captures go to your clipboard or a folder you choose. Your screenshots are yours.

Install

Getting started on macOS

Snap It is signed but not notarized (that requires a paid Apple Developer account), so the first launch is blocked with “could not verify … malware.” The app is safe — clear the quarantine flag once:

Terminal 
xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/snapit.app
Alternative: System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway

macOS is the primary platform. Windows and Linux builds are included in releases but are experimental and unverified on real hardware.
